Swami Vivekananda

Swami Vivekananda founder of the Ramakrishna Mission.

Indian philosophies of Vedanta and Yoga to the Western world, founder of the Ramakrishna Mission.

Here's a time line of Swami Vivekananda's life:

  • January 12, 1863, Kolkata, India
  • Education: Attended Presidency College and Scottish Church College in Kolkata Deeply influenced by his guru, Ramakrishna Paramahamsa.
  • Met Ramakrishna in 1881, which was a turning point in his life.
  • Advocate of Indian Culture: Played a significant role in the revival of Hinduism and Indian culture. Philosophical Contributions: Promoted Vedanta and Yoga as the essence of Indian spirituality.
  • Chicago Parliament of Religions, 1893 Historic Speech: Gained worldwide recognition with his speech beginning with "Sisters and brothers of America..."
    Impact: His speech highlighted the universal brotherhood and the spiritual heritage of India. .
  • Establishment of Ramakrishna Mission: Mission and Vision: Established in 1897, aimed at social service and spiritual upliftment.
    Legacy: Continues to be a major spiritual and humanitarian organization.
  • Teachings and Philosophy
    - Universal Religion: Advocated for universal acceptance and tolerance.
    - Self-realization and Service: Emphasized the importance of self-realization and serving humanity.
  • Literary Contributions
    - Books and Writings:Authored several books including "Raja Yoga," "Jnana Yoga," and "Karma Yoga."
    - Collected Works: His lectures, writings, and letters are compiled in "The Complete Works of Swami Vivekananda."
  • Legacy
    - Influence:Inspired numerous leaders and thinkers around the world.
    - National Youth Day:* His birthday, January 12, is celebrated as National Youth Day in India.
  • Death: July 4, 1902, Belur Math, India

"Swami Vivekananda inspired millions with his message of self-belief and relentless pursuit of goals. He taught that every individual holds immense potential and that true strength lies in conquering one's fears. His call to "Arise, awake, and stop not till the goal is reached" continues to motivate people to strive for excellence. His life exemplified the power of dedication, spirituality, and service to humanity."
